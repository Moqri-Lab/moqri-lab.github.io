About

AI for Health

Computational science for healthy aging

Moqri Lab is a computational health and aging research group at Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women's Hospital. The lab develops artificial intelligence and data-driven methods that integrate biomarkers, omics, and electronic health records to better understand health trajectories, predict patient outcomes, and support healthy aging.

Research Focus

Biomarkers, omics, and patient outcomes

The lab focuses on blood-based biomarkers of aging, biomarkers of Alzheimer's disease, and AI models for patient outcomes. Current work combines large-scale electronic health record data, multi-omics data, and clinical datasets to study disease risk, functional decline, and opportunities for earlier intervention.

Collaborative Science

From discovery to clinical impact

The group works across computational biology, clinical data science, aging research, and precision medicine. The long-term goal is to translate biomarker discoveries and predictive models into tools that can improve prevention, risk stratification, and care for aging-related conditions.
